Kaas Plateau Conservation

What We as NGO / Person Should Do? Can Do?

Remember that the plateau has many rare, endangered, and unique plant species found nowhere else in the world. It is our responsibility to protect these and avoid extinction.

By taking care of the plateau and enjoying its beauty responsibly, it will remain for years to come for our enjoyment and future generations. It is a prestigious World Heritage Site, recognized globally as a unique place. Damage to it would be a loss for us as a nation. Let's share the responsibility of conservation beyond the government.

  • Encourage sustainable tourism practices.
  • Participate in awareness campaigns for conservation.
  • Educate others about the significance of Kaas Plateau.
  • Volunteer for cleaning and preservation activities.

As an NGO

  • Avoid trips to Kaas until the conservation management plan is enforced by the Forest Department.
  • If you plan a trip:
    • Take permits and pay the fees decided by government departments.
    • Brief all tourists about the uniqueness and biodiversity of the plateau, as well as the care they need to take while visiting.
    • Organize sufficient escorts for the group, preferably one for every 10 people.
    • Ensure tourists follow rules of care and avoid harmful behaviors towards the plateau.
    • Park vehicles before the plateau and encourage walking on foot to preserve the environment.
    • Limit time on the plateau to avoid overcrowding and its negative impacts.

As an Individual

  • Do not walk on plants or flower beds.
  • Avoid plucking plants or flowers. They can only survive in the unique ambiance of the plateau.
  • Do not litter. Dispose of waste properly.
  • Avoid speaking loudly or playing loud music, as it disturbs the peaceful atmosphere.
  • Walk carefully on the plateau and avoid disturbing the ecosystem.
  • Respect nature and its fragile environment.
